Global Facts

Although some countries have made significant headway, this year’s Global Hunger Index shows little progress has been made towards reducing hunger on a global scale since 2015. The 2023 GHI score for the world is 18.3, which is considered moderate (for context, the world’s 2015 GHI score was 19.1).

In 2022, 148 million children under age 5 suffered from stunting. If current trends continue, it's projected that 1 in 5 children under age 5 will be affected by stunting by 2030​ (UN DESA)​.

Investment in agriculture has been declining. Government expenditure on agriculture relative to its contribution to GDP has decreased in most regions except Northern America and Europe, primarily due to the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic​ (UN Sustainable Development Goals)​.

In 2022, almost 60% of countries faced moderately to abnormally high food prices, largely due to the spillover effects of conflicts. This has had a significant impact on food accessibility and affordability​ (UN DESA)​.

The world produces enough food to feed all of its 8 billion people, yet 828 million people go hungry every day.

Of those 828 million, the World Food Program estimates that over 40% are facing acute levels of hunger.

Haiti, Mali, Sudan, South Sudan and the Occupied Palestinian Territories are the countries with the highest levels of hunger. We must act now to avoid catastrophe.

Nearly 1 in 10 people globally face hunger, with 2.4 billion people experiencing moderate to severe food insecurity in 2022. The percentage of the population affected by food insecurity has been rising, partly due to conflicts and economic disruptions​ (UN DESA)​​ (Data4SDGs)​.

As the largest and most populated continent, Asia is home to approximately 4.427 billion people. Unfortunately, more than 550 million people part of that population suffers from hunger.

Our Story

Established in 2024, Nourish Next has been a catalyst for positive change, powered by the dedication of our members and volunteers across India. Our commitment to impactful projects is driven by an unwavering passion to make a difference. We focus on community engagement, inclusive dialogue, education, and policy influence to combat hunger and promote nutrition. Join us in our mission to create meaningful change and uplift those in need, ensuring a brighter future for all. Together, we can nourish lives and build stronger communities.
